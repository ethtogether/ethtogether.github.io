Why do we need to talk about mental well being in times of Coronavirus?

Numerous studies have reported that social isolation has wreaked havoc on the public mental health. These studies indicate a significant increase in worries and anxiety, sleep deprivation, difficulty in concentrating at a task, loss of appetite, increased substance abuse are some of the most common mental effects of COVID-19 and related restrictions. Another indicator has been the crisis hotline numbers which have shown a spike in correlation with the increase in restrictions by the government and decline as soon as the government relaxed the restrictions. The situation is worse especially with students around the world. With the schools and universities being closed and all the education being given through remote learning, the majority of the students have experienced learning loss and indicated a lack of motivation. A published study in JMIR shows that out of 195 surveyed students, more than 80% students have indicated that they have felt the symptoms of degraded mental health in one form or the other. Another research shows the importance of physical activities for both physical and mental health, but with the recreational activity centers being closed the avenues of physical activities have decreased.

Feeling low? What to do?

Certainly, these uncertain times bring worries and anxiety to the best of us.

Here are certain things that you can do:

  • Exercise Regularly
  • Enrol into an online Yoga and Meditation class
  • Take a break and indulge in your hobbies
  • Have a balanced diet and avoid alcohol
  • Call a friend or a family member
